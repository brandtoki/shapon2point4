Cinematic Philosophy

The frame is not a window — it is a mirror held up to memory.

— Shapon, Director's Note

Every film I make begins with a cultural question: what does it mean to be Bangladeshi in the present moment, carrying the weight of a rich, complex past? The visual language of 70s Bengali cinema — its golden light, its unhurried pacing, its use of silence — is not nostalgia. It is a living grammar.

Through The Mrityukhudha Society, I pursue cinema that is both rooted and restless — deeply Bengali, yet speaking to something universal in the human experience.

MRITYUKHUDHA

The Collective

The Mrityukhudha
Society

The Mrityukhudha Society is more than a film production house — it is a space for cultural inquiry. "Mrityukhudha" (মৃত্যুক্ষুধা) — The Hunger of Death — is a name drawn from Nazrul Islam, one of Bangladesh's greatest literary voices.

Every project we undertake asks: what is worth preserving? What must be challenged? How does cinema serve as a record of a people's inner life?
